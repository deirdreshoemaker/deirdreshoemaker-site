Hi, I collide black holes for a living!

I am a physicist specializing in numerical relativity and gravitational wave astrophysics. My work focuses on solving Einstein's equations to unviel the dynamics of black hole mergers and to predict their emitted gravitational waves. I contribute to gravitational wave observatories like the current, fabulous, ground-based observatory LIGO and future ground and space-based missios, Cosmic Explorer and LISA. I am passionate about exploring the univers how gravity reveals its role in the Universe and mentoring the next generation of physicists.

Brief Bio

Deirdre received her B.S. in Physics, Astronomy, and Astrophysics from the Pennsylvania State University and her Ph.D. in Physics from the University of Texas at Austin. She was a postdoctoral fellow at Penn State and Cornell University before joining the faculty at Penn State in 2004. She moved to the School of Physics at Georgia Tech in 2008 and to the Department of Physics at the University of Texas at Austin in 2020. She has won the NSF Career award, was part of the Breack Through Prize given to the LIGO Scientific Collaboration, has served on the Astronomy and Astropysics Advisory Council, is a fellow of the American Physical Society, and has held several elected roles in APS DGRAV. Her current favorite activiity is being a memebr of the LISA Science Team.
